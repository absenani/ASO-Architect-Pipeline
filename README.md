Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma(PDAC) is a leading cause of cancer-related mortality, partially because of the notoriously difficult druggable nature of the KRAS protein. PDAC is aggressive and most current treatments fail to target the KRAS protein because of its structure. The question that was posed was, “Can an antisense oligonucleotide be engineered to bypass the structure of the protein and successfully target and inhibit its synthesis at the mRNA level?” We hypothesized that the designed drug would bind to the transcript and inhibit synthesis of the KRAS protein. The methodology used in the experiment was a computational pipeline including Biopython for its structural accessibility analysis(4), and IDT OligoAnalyzer to check for thermodynamic stability when binding to the mRNA. The result was a 20-mer ASO that targets the c.35G>T mutation with a binding affinity (Delta G) of -16.22 kcal/mol. RNAfold predicted a 70% accessibility at the target site for the drug, and BLASTn confirmed zero off-target matches in the human transcript database. The findings reveal a digital blueprint for a highly specialized drug candidate that could circumvent protein structures and bind to the transcript of proteins, treating previously undruggable drivers of cancer. There were three iterations of the final script. Each is published in the releases.
